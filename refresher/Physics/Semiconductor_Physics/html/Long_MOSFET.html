The Ideal MOSFET

The simplified diagram of the general MOSFET above is an ideal MOSFET diagram as below:

Simlification is under following assumption:

  1. Ideal MOS structure
  2. One Dimensional current flow, this assumption comes from the "Long" MOSFET assumption
  3. Uniform doping
  4. Maxwell-Boltzman statistics, (the doping are non-degenerate, which is simplified assumption from Fermi statistics)
  5. Inversion layer transport is by drift alone, which is dominately by the E-field with in the channel. This is electron transport by diffusion is significantly smaller.
  6. Channel electrons have constant mobility, the - is in the linear proportion regime, and NOT saturated.
  7. Neglect body effect ( does change along the channel region ( direction))
  8. No parasitic resistances, parasitic resistances can come from quasi-neutral region.
  9. Device scales perfectly in direction
  10. No effect of substrate
  11. Reverse biased PN junction leakage is negligible
  12. Fields are low - no impact ionization
